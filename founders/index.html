Skincare with nothing to hide

Read every label. Ours included.

It started with a question I couldn't stop asking at the back of every label: what is this — and is it going to hurt me?

I went looking — really looking — at what's actually in the things we put on our skin and lips every day. And the deeper I got, the more it felt like I was being handed a bad choice. Fear on one side, screaming that everything's out to get you. Junk on the other, quietly packed with stuff nobody can pronounce. And the handful of “clean” brands that seemed honest? Boring. Beige. Like I had to pick between safe and cool.

I didn't want to pick. I wanted both.

So I built Savage Skin: clean formulas that actually work, in packaging you'd be proud to leave on your shelf — with nothing to hide. Just the truth about what's in it and what it's doing for you, written in plain English a human can actually read.

No fear-mongering. No mystery fillers. Just the truth about what's in it — and what it's doing for you.
/ Why Lips First

We started with lips on purpose.

You swallow a little of your lip products every day, for years. So lips are where clean matters most — and where it gets faked the least.

Our hero, Lip Service, is held to one simple test: nothing in it you'd be afraid to swallow. Daily balm, overnight mask, and a hydration-plump in one tube. Skincare for your lips.

/ Radical Transparency

The label is the flex.

Most brands hide behind a wall of unpronounceable filler. We do the opposite. Every ingredient gets explained in plain English — what it is, what it does, and why it earned its spot.

  • Plain-English labels. What each ingredient actually does — no chemistry degree required.
  • No mystery fillers. If it's in there, it's there for a reason we'll tell you.
  • No fear-marketing. The truth instead — even when the truth is unglamorous.
  • No invented reviews, no fake results. Ever. Verified reviews go up only when real customers leave them.
/ What Savage Means

Savage is an attitude — not an ingredient. It's bold, not harsh. Brave, not cold. You don't owe anyone a smile to be stunning.

Picture the women who are fierce and beautiful at the same time — who don't shrink, don't perform, and don't apologize for taking up space. That's the whole energy. Savage is my last name — and it's the standard we hold every formula to.
